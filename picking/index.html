Find Your Fastest
Picking Motion.

Most guitarists hit a speed wall and don't know why. The motion that worked at slow tempos only carries you so far. The speed burst is how you find the right-hand motion that actually scales, by learning from yourself instead of copying someone else's hand. Work through the seven steps below, then log a few sessions to track real progress.

Time 10 min / session
Level Intermediate
Equipment Guitar, metronome optional
Based on The speed burst method
Step 01

The Phrase

Ascending sixes on the D and G strings. This isolates a single-string motion plus one string crossing, which is enough data to see how your right hand actually wants to move.

Ascending Sixes · D & G Strings 6 notes · alternate picking
⊓ Down · V Up · Note 4 marks the string change
Step 02

The Burst Process

Build up two notes at a time. As fast as you can, with good tone, no tension, and clean synchronization. Pause between bursts. Hit each step four to five clean reps in a row before moving on.

0 / 7
01
Two notes
Play 1 2 as one fast burst. Pause. Repeat. Tone clean, hand relaxed.
02
Add the third note
Burst 1 2 3. Same rules. If it falls apart, slow the burst down a hair, not the technique.
03
Isolate the string crossing
The string change is at note 4. Burst 3 4 only. Two notes again, but now across strings. Picking stays correct: down up.
04
Approach from before
Burst 2 3 4. The crossing now has a runway. Note 2 is an upstroke, so start on an upstroke.
05
Through the crossing
Burst 1 2 3 4. Full run-up through the string change. Watch for hidden tension in the forearm.
06
Five notes
Burst 1 2 3 4 5. You now have enough data to see your real fast motion forming.
07
All six
Burst 1 2 3 4 5 6. The full pattern. This is the motion you'll reverse engineer at slow tempos.
Step 03

The Rules

Without these, the burst becomes random tension building. With them, you're learning from yourself.

01
Always pause
Don't loop bursts back to back. The pause is what stops you from grinding tension into the motion.
02
Tone first
If the tone breaks down, the burst doesn't count. Fast and ugly is just fast and ugly.
03
Keep picking direction correct
When you start mid-phrase, the picking has to match the original. Don't reset to a downstroke just because it feels easier.
04
It won't look like your heroes
Wrist, forearm, fingers, hybrid. Different players use different motions and all sound great. Find yours.
05
Match slow practice to the burst
The whole point. Whatever motion the burst reveals, that's the motion you reinforce at slow tempos.
06
Diagnostic, not a fix
This shows you the destination. The slow work is still what gets you there.
Step 04

Log Your Bursts

Track your max clean burst on this phrase across sessions. After two weeks of honest reps you'll see whether the motion you found is holding up under repetition. Saves to your browser.
